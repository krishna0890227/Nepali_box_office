Nepali Movie History

Nepali cinema began in 1951 with Satya Harishchandra, but Aama (1964) marked its true start. The industry grew in the 1970s-90s with films like Maitighar. The 2000s brought digital advancements, and the 2010s saw a revival with hits like Loot and Kabaddi, expanding Nepal’s film presence globally.

Nepali Movie Talk

With improved storytelling, cinematography, and digital advancements, the industry is growing globally. Festivals and streaming platforms are helping Nepali films reach wider audiences, promoting unique local stories worldwide

Enjoy Nepali Culture

Nepali movies beautifully showcase culture, traditions, and values. Films like Balidan, Kalo Pothi, and Sano Sansar highlight Nepal’s heritage, festivals, and social issues through compelling storytelling.

Movie Promotion

Nepali movies are gaining global recognition through international film festivals, streaming platforms, and social media. Films like The Red Phallus and Kalo Pothi showcase Nepal’s culture, attracting worldwide audiences and appreciation.
